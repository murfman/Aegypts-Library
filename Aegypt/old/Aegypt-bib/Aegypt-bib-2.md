# Introduction

The goal is to list all of the books and authors mentioned throughout *Ægypt*, or *The Solitudes*, by chapter. A complete list of those authors are listed at the end in alphabetical order by the first name. If a chapter is missing below, then it likely did not have a book or authors mentioned within it. Books listed in **bold** are fictional works created by Crowley. The *Authors Mentioned* subsections list authors that were mentioned but without the context of a book.

In most cases I tried to cite a work that would have been appropriate for the timeframeof the story in which it is mentioned. In some cases, I have not been able to find a suitable edition and so cite a modern edition instead. Those will eventually be cleaned up to reflect either a copy of the work from the 16th century or the late 1970s as I find them.

For any corrections or comments the reader can email me directly at [murfman@pm.me](mailto:murfman@pm.me).

The characters it was written in started immediately at the top of the first page without any heading, as though this were perhaps not the first page at all. Doctor Dee picked up the lamp and bent close over the page. Mr. Talbot turned the heavy worm-holed leaf. The second page was the same: a solid block of characters from top to bottom. It is in a cipher, the doctor said. I can read a cipher, if it hides a language that I know."

The identical caricature marks also the conditions under which the second edition of the eighteenth Brumaire is issued."

I'm simply going to list the most likely candidates here: "The Book of Clocks" by David Thompson (1971), "Clocks and Watches" by Eric Bruton (1973), "The Art of the Clockmaker" by Tardy (early 1970s), "The Lore of the Clock" by G. Again this could be either intentional by Crowley subtly showing the differences between our world and the book's, or he simply could have chosen a date. Personally, I think it is likely the former.
