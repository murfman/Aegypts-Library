\lettrine[lines=4,findent=3pt]{T}{his} is a continuation of my attempt at listing out the various authors and books mentioned within the John Crowley's Ægypt Tetralogy, This volume consists of the references for \textit{Love \& Sleep} in particular. Fictitious authors and books have been placed in bold typeface for easy identification, and a bibliography of real and fictitious books are listed at the end of the document.

For those that care, this project was originally done in RStudio to make use of their excellent Rmarkdown code and integration abilities, especially those concerned with BibTeX files. Once I finished it there a friend \textit{(you know who you are James\ldots)} mentioned it would be cool to have as a zine instead of a flat PDF, so I went way down that rabbit-hole before coming back to sanity. Well sort of\dots I'm using \hologo{LuaLaTeX} now, I started with \LaTeX{} because I was familiar enough with it and it is ridiculously powerful, two things I needed if I was going to print physical zines. So, after much trial and tribulation what started out as a text file then migrated into Markdown then Rmarkdown, then to \LaTeX{}, and finally to \hologo{LuaLaTeX}; and from letter size to half-letter zine, and now back to letter, which I'm sure highlights my own inability to commit more than anything else. Speaking of which we won't even begin to go into how many small tweaks, rebuilds, custom commands, \tikzlogo glyphs, etc. have been added, removed, created, and ultimately forgotten about to only be rediscovered at 2\textsc{A.M.} after wondering why things just didn't compile correctly. 

It really is a labor of love at this stage, or a clear sign of a deficiency of some sort personally.
