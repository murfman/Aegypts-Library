[^8]: In this same section he has Pierce recognizing Giordano Bruno

    refers to later in Nati-15.

[^9]: Named by Crowley using the variant "Dr. Hannibal."

[^10]: Named by Crowley as "Abbot Joachim of Flora."

[^12]: Noted as being a book of ghost stories by Fellowes Kraft.

[^13]: The bible is used by the pastor at Boney's funeral where Job 1:21
    is quoted. As an aside this led to a look into the possible origins
    of the particular denomination that Boney belonged to, here named
    "The Danish Brethren." The best I can determine now is that it was a
    splinter group formed after the first or second schism of the Bible
    Student Movement in the late 1920s, prior to gaining the name
    Jehovah's Witnesses.

[^14]: Pierce never mentions the book by name here but instead by
    Burton's name and references *"Amor hereos"* which Barr says
    *"...was a part of melancholy."*
